About me

I am a Full-Stack Developer with a strong foundation in software engineering and a long-standing passion for technology and music.

My interest in programming began at an early age and continued through my academic studies, including a specialization in Computer Science during high school and advanced programming training as part of the Atuda (Yud-Gimel) program. During this time, I gained extensive experience in object-oriented programming using languages such as Java, C#, and Assembly. Later on, I completed a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Reichman University.

In my current role as a Full-Stack Developer, I design and build web applications using Angular and C#, focusing on creating scalable, reliable, and user-focused solutions.

Alongside my technical career, music has remained an important part of my personal and creative development. I began playing the trumpet in high school and later performed with the Kleiman Big Band at the Jerusalem Academy of Music. After completing my degree, I continued my musical education at the Rimon School of Music, where I further developed skills in composition, performance, piano, and vocal work.

I also have extensive experience in education and mentoring. I have worked as a Computer Science and Mathematics teacher, tutor, and teaching assistant at both Reichman University and Zeev School in Herzliya. These roles strengthened my ability to communicate complex concepts clearly and support the growth of others.

I am motivated by opportunities to solve meaningful problems, continuously expand my skills, and contribute to impactful technological solutions.

AWS project

License Plate Recognition on AWS

Upload a car image, detect its plate number, and then query the database for the result. The full system is deployed in AWS.

Cloud-deployed system

From image upload to searchable and editable results

An Angular frontend lets users upload vehicle images and query detections. A Flask API stores images in S3, queues processing with SQS, an EC2 worker runs OpenALPR, and the detected plate data is saved to MySQL on RDS and returned to the app.

View on GitHub

Coming soon: a live demo here!

Technologies used

Angular Python Flask AWS EC2 AWS S3 AWS SQS AWS RDS MySQL OpenALPR Nginx CloudFormation

AWS flow

Car image User uploads a vehicle photo.
→
Angular + Flask The frontend sends the image to the API.
→
S3 + SQS The image is stored and queued for processing.
→
EC2 + OpenALPR The worker detects plate number and color.
→
RDS + app UI Results are saved, queried, and can be edited.
